Meet Assembly Member Erik Dilan

For more than two decades, Erik Martin Dilan has been a steady, experienced voice for North Brooklyn – fighting for safer streets, stable housing, and the resources working families need to stay and thrive in the communities they call home.

A lifelong Brooklynite and graduate of New York City public schools, Erik brings deep neighborhood roots and practical legislative experience to Albany every day.

  • Serving in the New York State Assembly since 2015, representing the 54th District.
  • Chair of the Assembly Committee on Correction and member of key committees on housing, energy, insurance, and the budget.
  • Former New York City Council Member with a long record of neighborhood-level problem solving.
  • Trusted advocate for tenants, seniors, and working families across North Brooklyn.

Record of Service

Grounded in Brooklyn. Focused on Results.

In the Assembly
As Chair of the Committee on Correction and a member of the Housing, Energy, Insurance, and Ways & Means Committees, Erik helps shape the state budget and statewide policy on housing, public safety, and core services for working families.
In City Government
Before joining the Assembly, Erik served on the New York City Council, where he focused on land use, neighborhood planning, and local investments in schools, parks, and senior centers.
In the Community
Erik grew up in North Brooklyn and has worked with tenant leaders, block associations, faith institutions, and small businesses to keep long-time residents in their homes and strengthen the fabric of the district.
Erik’s Priorities

Putting North Brooklyn Families First

In Albany, Erik’s work is guided by a simple commitment: government should make it easier—not harder—for families to build stable, secure lives in the neighborhoods they love. His agenda focuses on housing, public safety, and economic security.

Erik has been at the forefront of efforts to strengthen tenant protections, expand rent stabilization, and fund the construction and preservation of truly affordable housing. He has backed tougher penalties for landlord harassment, pushed to curb speculative real-estate practices, and fought for investments that keep long-time residents rooted in North Brooklyn.

As Chair of the Committee on Correction, Erik has led oversight of jails and prisons while advancing reforms that improve safety, transparency, and reentry supports. He supports targeted public safety investments, youth programs, and violence interruption initiatives that reduce crime and build trust between communities and law enforcement—because families deserve both safety and justice.

Erik continues to champion legislation that lowers everyday costs and expands opportunity for working people—from childcare and senior services to consumer protections and fair workplace standards. He has supported policies that make credit reporting more transparent, ensure state programs reach families in need, and keep North Brooklyn’s working- and middle-class residents from being priced out of the city they built.
