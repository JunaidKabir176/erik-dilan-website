Erik Dilan on the issues

Fighting for Fairness and Stability

Assembly Member Erik Martin Dilan has spent his career focused on keeping families housed, improving public safety, and making sure working people in North Brooklyn and across New York have a fair shot. Below are some of the core areas where he is leading and delivering.

Assembly Member Erik Dilan has built his public life around strengthening housing stability, fighting displacement, and protecting tenants from abuse. He supports legislation to criminalize landlord harassment of rent-regulated tenants, rein in speculative flipping, and broaden protections for vulnerable renters, including New Yorkers with disabilities and service animals. Erik treats housing as a basic foundation for family life and community, and he pushes policies that preserve affordability and curb predatory practices.

As Chair of the Assembly Committee on Correction, Erik Dilan leads efforts to bring transparency, accountability, and a basic standard of dignity to New York’s correctional system. He works to strengthen oversight of facilities, improve conditions for incarcerated people, and expand reentry supports so individuals can successfully return to their communities. Erik’s approach pairs public safety with fairness — advancing reforms that reduce recidivism, support rehabilitation, and uphold civil rights.

Erik Dilan champions policies that ease pressure on working families and guard against unfair economic practices. His legislative work includes pushing for greater transparency in credit reporting, limiting abusive real-estate speculation, and protecting residents from financial exploitation. Through his committee roles, he supports affordable utilities, fair insurance practices, and economic policies that reduce burdens on low- and middle-income households.

Erik is a strong advocate for disability rights, especially in housing. He has sponsored legislation that broadens reasonable accommodation protections and shields people with disabilities from discrimination and harassment. That includes protections for tenants who rely on service or support animals. His work is driven by a clear belief: every New Yorker deserves dignity, safety, and equal treatment under the law.

Rooted in decades of service in North Brooklyn, Erik Dilan continues to fight for investments that uplift working families and strengthen neighborhood infrastructure. He champions funding for local schools, senior services, community programs, and public-safety partnerships, while pushing to ensure historically underserved areas receive their fair share of state resources. Erik’s focus is simple: government should work for the people it serves and measurably improve quality of life.
