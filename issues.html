Erik Dilan on the issues

Fighting for Fairness and Stability

Assembly Member Erik Martin Dilan has spent his career focused on keeping families housed, improving public safety, and making sure working people in North Brooklyn and across New York have a fair shot. Below are some of the core areas where he is leading and delivering.

Affordable housing remains scarce throughout the city. And at a time when major budget shortfalls are imminent for New York, Erik knows that housing stability must remain a top priority. Erik brings proven leadership on housing from his 12 years in the New York City Council, where he served as Chair of the Housing & Buildings Committee. In this capacity, Erik was able to build consensus on legislation that shaped citywide housing and construction policy. Erik remains committed to advancing housing policy through sponsoring legislation that supports vulnerable people. With an eye for supporting policies that will bring lower & middle income & rentals to NY’s housing market. Erik will continue to fight hard to keep private equity & investors out of the market for small homes and rentals.

As Chair of the Assembly’s Committee on Correction, Erik led the passage of the correction reform law of 2025, but this is just a start to improving independent transparency and accountability to NYS prisons. Working to improve conditions, safety and reentry support is important and will be the next area of focus of his work. For decades many New Yorkers have received criminal sentences disproportionate to the crime they committed, leading to a long time in prison. Supporting and pushing for passage of the Second Look Act and Earned Time Act to help alleviate the negative impacts of unjust long sentences.

The well-being of our community's future generation is key to the long-term strength and resilience of our neighborhood. As a father of three, Erik has experienced firsthand the struggles associated with finding reliable and affordable childcare. He supports funding for expanded universal pre 2K, 3K and Pre-K in this year’s state budget. Expanding access to younger children has proven to enhance educational outcomes later in life for kids, for parents it will reduce the cost of Childcare and allow for them to work knowing their kids are safe. Work with City Hall to re-open the recently shuttered, Audrey Johnson and John Coker Day Care centers. This year, the Governor wants to invest over two billion dollars for these programs, much of it, will be administered by City Hall. If childcare is going to be expanded, Bushwick will need these facilities reopened in order to maximize the number of slots. I look forward to working with City Hall to achieve this and will put up State Capital funds to help with modernization and renovation of these facilities. Let’s get this done!

NY’s health care system is under attack by Washington D.C. Republicans when they passed H.R.1, creating a deficit in Medicaid in the billions. Erik will fight to protect Medicaid—our entire health care system is dependent on it.

Our focus

  • Defend safety-net and public hospitals so they can continue to serve our neighborhoods.
  • Work to fix dangerous staffing shortages by supporting nurses and frontline workers.
  • Close insurance coverage gaps for young people, gig workers, immigrants and freelancers.
  • Protect the Essential Plan.
  • Invest in community health centers, mental health services and harm reduction.
  • Treat substance use as a public health issue, not a criminal issue.
  • Expand telehealth and modernize care delivery.
